Outdoor & Pool Tile

Pool Decks & Outdoor Spaces
Built for Coastal Living

Coastal Carolina living is outdoor living. Your pool deck, covered porch, and outdoor kitchen are extensions of your home — and they deserve the same quality of tile work as any interior space. At Haven Tile & Stone, we specialize in outdoor tile installations that look incredible and hold up to everything the Carolina coast throws at them: heat, humidity, salt air, pool chemicals, and foot traffic.

Donovan has been installing outdoor tile along the Brunswick County coast for over 15 years. He knows which materials are built for this environment and which ones fail — and he'll guide you to selections that will look beautiful for decades, not just seasons.

What We Install Outdoors

  • Pool deck tile — slip-resistant porcelain in a wide range of tones and textures designed for pool surrounds
  • Pool coping tile — precision-cut bullnose and coping tiles that frame the pool edge cleanly
  • Covered patio and screened porch tile — large-format porcelain that flows from interior to exterior
  • Outdoor kitchen and grill station tile — heat-resistant backsplashes and counter surrounds
  • Exterior stair tile — non-slip treads and risers for elevated decks and landings
  • Walkway and entry tile — stone-look porcelain paths that complement the home's architecture
  • Shower surrounds for pool baths — outdoor shower tile that handles daily wet/dry cycles
  • Hot tub and spa surrounds — waterproof tile installations around above-ground and in-ground spas

Materials for the Outdoors

  • Outdoor-rated porcelain — dense, frost-resistant, low-absorption tile rated for exterior use in coastal climates
  • Natural travertine and slate — timeless stone options with natural slip resistance when properly finished
  • Wood-look porcelain planks — the look of hardwood with the durability of ceramic for covered areas
  • Large-format slabs (24x48, 24x24) — fewer grout lines, cleaner look, easier long-term maintenance
  • Textured and brushed finishes — added grip without sacrificing aesthetics for high-traffic wet zones

The Haven Tile & Stone Process

Outdoor tile installations require more planning than interior work — drainage slopes, expansion joints, proper substrate for freeze/thaw cycles (even mild ones), and waterproofing under outdoor shower areas. Donovan addresses all of this before the first tile goes down.

We start with a free consultation, provide a detailed written estimate, and execute on schedule — leaving your outdoor space ready to enjoy.
